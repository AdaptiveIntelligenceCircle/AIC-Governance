## Purpose

This document defines **how governance documents may change**
without enabling capture, drift, or erosion of core principles.

Amendment is allowed.
Corruption is not.

---

## What Can Be Amended

The following may be amended:
- Governance procedures
- DAO structures
- Review processes
- Documentation and clarification

Amendments must improve clarity, resilience, or accountability.

---

## What Cannot Be Amended

The following are **explicitly unamendable**:
- Immutable Principles
- Ethical Constraints that enforce those principles
- The right to sunset systems
- The requirement for human override

Any attempt to amend these is invalid by definition.

---

## Amendment Process

### 1. Proposal
An amendment proposal must include:
- Precise changes
- Rationale
- Ethical impact analysis
- Failure scenarios
- Reversibility assessment

### 2. Review
Mandatory review by:
- Multiple DAOs
- At least one human-centered DAO
- Governance maintainers

Speed is explicitly discouraged.

### 3. Objection Window
A minimum objection period must be observed.
Silence does not equal consent.

### 4. Resolution
Consensus is preferred.
If consensus fails, non-action is the default outcome.

---

## Ethical Veto on Amendments

Any ethical veto:
- Immediately halts the amendment
- Requires written justification
- Cannot be overridden by majority

Amending ethics requires ethical permission.

---

## Amendment Memory

All amendments must:
- Be permanently recorded
- Include original and revised text
- Preserve dissenting opinions

Historical amnesia is prohibited.

---

## Anti-Capture Clause

If amendment activity:
- Accelerates under external pressure
- Favors centralized authority
- Reduces transparency or reversibility

it must be paused and reviewed as a security incident.

---

## Final Clause

> A constitution that is easy to change
> is not a constitution.
> It is a suggestion.

AIC chooses difficulty on purpose.
